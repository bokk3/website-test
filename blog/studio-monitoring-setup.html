Getting Started with Studio Monitoring

Boris Truyens 8 min read

When it comes to music production, your monitoring setup is arguably the most critical part of your studio. No matter how great your gear is, if you can't hear your mix accurately, you'll never achieve professional results.

The Basics of Monitor Placement

The classic "equilateral triangle" setup is your starting point. Position your monitors and listening position to form an equilateral triangle, with your head forming one point and each speaker forming the other two points.

Room Acoustics Matter

Even the most expensive monitors won't perform well in a poorly treated room. Here are the key areas to address:

  • First reflection points
  • Bass traps in corners
  • Ceiling and floor reflections
  • Rear wall treatment

Common Monitoring Mistakes

In my years of studio consulting, I've seen these mistakes repeatedly:

  1. Placing monitors too close to walls
  2. Incorrect monitor height
  3. Mismatched monitor levels
  4. Ignoring room modes

Monitor Calibration

Proper calibration is essential for consistent mixes. Use a calibrated measurement microphone and room correction software to identify and address frequency response issues.

"Your monitors are your window into your mix. Make sure that window is crystal clear."

Recommended Tools

Here are some essential tools for optimizing your monitoring setup:

  • Room measurement microphone
  • Acoustic treatment panels
  • Monitor isolation pads
  • SPL meter
